Exercise Guide

1. Squat

  • Start Position: Stand with your feet shoulder-width apart, toes pointing slightly outward.
  • Movement: Lower your body by bending your knees and pushing your hips back, as if sitting into an imaginary chair.
  • Depth: Aim to lower until your thighs are parallel to the ground, keeping your back straight and chest up.
  • Return: Push through your heels, engaging your glutes and quads, to return to the starting position.
  • Repetition: Perform 3 sets of 10-15 reps, gradually increasing as your strength improves.
  • Targeted Muscles: Primarily works the quadriceps, hamstrings, and glutes.

2. Plank

  • Start Position: Begin in a push-up position with your arms straight and shoulders directly above your wrists.
  • Body Alignment: Keep your body in a straight line from head to heels, engaging your core muscles.
  • Hold: Maintain the position for 30 seconds to 1 minute, or as long as you can while keeping proper form.
  • Breathing: Breathe deeply and evenly throughout the exercise.
  • Repetition: Repeat for 3 sets, gradually increasing the duration over time.
  • Targeted Muscles: Focuses on the core muscles, including the rectus abdominis and transverse abdominis.

3. Push-Up

  1. Start Position: Start in a plank position with your hands slightly wider than shoulder-width apart.
  2. Descent: Lower your body by bending your elbows, keeping them close to your sides.
  3. Depth: Lower until your chest nearly touches the ground, maintaining a straight line from head to heels.
  4. Ascent: Push through your palms to return to the starting position.
  • Repetition: Aim for 3 sets of 8-12 reps, adjusting as needed based on your strength level.
  • Targeted Muscles: Targets the chest muscles (pectoralis major), shoulders (deltoids), and triceps.

Remember to prioritize proper form over quantity and gradually progress as your strength improves. Always consult with a fitness professional or healthcare provider before starting a new exercise program.
